The Week In Crypto Policy: Bitcoin Reserves, VP Harris New Advisor, Sanctions Amendment to NDAA

George Leonardo

Aug 2, 2024   9 min read

While the August recess may be starting up, crypto policy news showed no signs of slowing down.

Top Points

  • Senator Cynthia Lummis (R-WY) introduced a bill to direct Treasury to buy 1 million Bitcoin over five years and hold the supply for at least 20 years.
  • Vice President Harris's presidential campaign added a new advisor: Treasury Under Secretary Brian Nelson, who, in his role at Treasury, has overseen FinCEN's proposed rule on cryptocurrency mixers and OFAC's sanctioning of Tornado Cash.
  • Senators Mark Warner (D-VA) and Marco Rubio (R-FL) filed an NDAA amendment including a provision to authorize Treasury to stop U.S. persons from transacting with certain foreign digital asset transaction facilitators who knowingly facilitate sanctions evasions, terrorist financing, or money laundering.

Senator Cynthia Lummis BITCOIN Act of 2024

On Wednesday, Senator Cynthia Lummis (R-WY) introduced a new bill to direct the U.S. Treasury Department to buy 200,000 Bitcoins per year, for five years, for a total purchase of 1 million Bitcoin (BTC). 5.

Treasury would have to hold purchased BTC for a minimum of 20 years in a Strategic Bitcoin Reserve. Id.

To pay for the purchases, the bill would:

  • lower the Feds discretionary surplus fund from $6.8 billion to $2.4 billion,
  • direct Treasury to use net earnings remitted by the Federal Reserve (for FY 2025-2029, up to first $6 billion), and
  • revalue the Federal Reserve banks gold certificates to the current fair market value. 9.

Any Bitcoin already held by a federal agency would also be added to the Strategic Bitcoin Reserve. 7.

The bill would also establish a proof of reserve system whereby the Secretary would publish quarterly reports verifying the Strategic Bitcoin Reserve's holdings and transaction history. 6.

What's Next?

The bill has been referred to the Senate Banking Committee, where it will likely remain stalled for the remainder of the 118th Congress. So far, there are no cosponsors.

Treasury Undersecretary Nelson To Advise VP Harris Campaign

Background

On Tuesday, VP Kamala Harris's team announced that Brian Nelson will serve on her presidential campaign as a policy advisor.

Brian Nelson currently serves as Treasury's Under Secretary for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence.

This position oversees Treasurys Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN), and other illicit finance-focused agencies.

Under Secretary Nelsons Record on Crypto

Under Nelsons tenure, two initiatives by FinCEN and OFAC have raised concerns amongst the crypto community:

  • (1) a FinCEN proposed rule on crypto mixing, and
  • (2) the sanctioning of Tornado Cash-associated wallet addresses that hold immutable software.

(1) Proposed Rule on CVC Mixing

  • Background: In October, FinCEN proposed:
    • (1) designating transactions that involve cryptocurrency mixing and a foreign jurisdiction to be of primary money laundering concern and
    • (2) imposing additional recordkeeping and reporting requirements on U.S. financial institutions for transactions they know, suspect, or have reason to suspect involve cryptocurrency mixing and a foreign jurisdiction nexus.
    • Proposed Rule (Comment period closed in January.)
  • Concerns: Industry has pushed back against the proposal, primarily arguing it is overly expansive (e.g., defines CVC mixing to include pooling or aggregating crypto from multiple persons, wallets, addresses, or accounts) and threatens financial privacy. More detailed summary of concerns in 1.26 Newsletter here.

(2) OFAC Sanctions Tornado Cash Software

  • Background: On August 8, 2022, Treasury sanctioned the URL and various Ethereum addresses associated with Tornado Cash, a decentralized cryptocurrency mixer, including addresses holding the immutable smart contract software that allowed users to obfuscate their transaction history on the Ethereum blockchain.
    • In a press release accompanying the sanctions, Under Secretary Nelson stated:
      • Despite public assurances otherwise, Tornado Cash has repeatedly failed to impose effective controls designed to stop it from laundering funds for malicious cyber actors on a regular basis and without basic measures to address its risks. Treasury will continue to aggressively pursue actions against mixers that launder virtual currency for criminals and those who assist them.
  • Concerns: Industry and lawmakers have criticized OFACs decision to sanction code.
    • For example, Coin Center sued Treasury in federal court, arguing: (1) OFAC lacked the statutory authority to sanction the Tornado Cash smart contracts because Tornado Cash is not a person and the smart contracts are not property under existing sanctions law, and (2) OFAC violated First Amendment free speech protections by sanctioning code. A District Court ruled against their arguments last October, but Coin Center plans to appeal.
    • Also, GOP Whip Tom Emmer expressed concerns with the sanctions in a letter to Treasury Secretary Yellen, noting:
      • These sanctions are unique, however, in that they were not levied against a person or an entity, but against privacy-enabling code.

Legislative Proposals

In November 2023, Treasury submitted legislative proposals to Congress aimed at disrupting terrorist financing networks, largely in response to Hamas's October 7th attack on Israel.

The proposals were submitted by Deputy Treasury Secretary Wally Adeyemo, but because they cover issues and agencies directly under Under Secretary Brian Nelson's purview, they may still shed light on Nelson's crypto policy views.

  • Text: Letter & Memo with Proposals.

Most notably, one of Treasury's proposals would subject wallet providers, validator nodes, and decentralized finance services to the BSAs AML/CFT requirements, mirroring sections of Senator Warrens Digital Asset Anti-Money Laundering Act.

Other legislative proposals submitted by Treasury included: clarifying OFAC's authority to sanction blockchain nodes, networks or other elements of cryptocurrency transactions, clarifying that OFAC's sanctions authority extends to USD-backed stablecoins and foreign entities with U.S. touch points, and clarifying that FinCEN's jurisdiction under the BSA extends to foreign entities with U.S. touch points.

Whats Next?

Its unclear to what extent Nelson will push VP Harris to support the above policies, but the fact that he was tapped early to work on the campaign signals he may be one carryover from the Biden administration to a potential Harris administration.

Senator Elizabeth Warren (D-MA) voiced support for the hire, telling Politico: Its always good to have someone advising our leaders about the threats to national security that come from holes in our anti-money laundering rules.

Quick Hits

Legislation

Sanctions

  • Senators Mark Warner (D-VA) and Marco Rubio (R-FL) have filed an NDAA amendment (SA 2274) to attach the Intelligence Authorization Act for FY 2025 to the NDAA.
    • SA 2274 includes the Terrorist Financing Prevention Act, which would authorize Treasury to stop U.S. persons from transacting with certain foreign digital asset transaction facilitators who knowingly facilitate significant financial transactions with, or launder money for, sanctioned entities, persons, or terrorists.
    • A foreign digital asset transaction facilitator would be defined as:
      • Any foreign person that, as determined by the Secretary, controls, operates, or makes available a digital asset protocol or similar facility, or otherwise materially assists in the purchase, sale, exchange, custody, or other transaction involving an exchange or transfer of value using digital assets.
      • More detailed summary of bill in June 7 Newsletter here.
    • Votes on the Senate NDAA and pending amendments are not expected until after August recess. (Pro Tip: Follow @connorobrienNH for NDAA news).
    • It's unlikely the crypto sanctions language makes it through the Senate and Republican-controlled House as drafted and gets signed into law.

Market Structure

  • Hopes for a markup of Senate Ag Chairwoman Debbie Stabenow's (D-MI) crypto market structure bill slipped to September, as Sen. Stabenow continues to look for Republican support. See full statement below:
  • Ranking Member John Boozman (R-AR) said the bill is moving in the right direction, but needs more work. See full statement here.

Bitcoin 2024 Conference Highlights

  • Former President Trump vowed to support Bitcoin mining, protect self-custody, fire SEC Chair Gensler, create a strategic reserve of Bitcoin, oppose a U.S. CBDC, and establish an advisory council to create transparent, pro-crypto rules.
    • Speech.
  • RFK Jr. pledged to make Bitcoin transactions nontaxable, classify the federal government's 200,00 seized BTC as a strategic asset, and incentivize green energy production through Bitcoin mining.
    • Speech.
  • In a panel with Sen. Lummis, Senator Tim Scott (R-SC) - the leading Republican on Senate Banking Committee - said he would promote Bitcoin and blockchain innovation in the U.S. if he becomes Senate Banking Chairman next Congress.
    • Panel.
  • Rep. Wiley Nickel (D-NC) said Trump is full of it, that he's working behind the scenes to convince VP Harris to "reset" the Biden Administration's crypto positions (see letter below), and argued that politicizing crypto will be counterproductive for the space.

Letter to DNC

  • 14 Democrats in Congress sent a letter to the DNC urging Chair Harrison and Prospective Presidential Candidates to take a forward-looking approach to digital assets and blockchain.
  • Specifically, the letter urges the DNC to:
    • (1) Include Pro-Digital asset language in the Party's platform;
    • (2) Select a VP Candidate sophisticated in digital asset policy;
    • (3) Select a pro-innovation SEC Chair;
    • (4) Engage with industry experts.
      • 14 Democrat candidates for office also signed the letter.
      • No U.S. Senators signed the letter.

Campaign Funding

  • Pro-crypto group Fairshake becomes largest super PAC of election cycle, raising over $200 million.
    • Article by James Hunt; The Block.

Crypto in the Courts

Song a Day Mann v. the SEC

  • Jonathan Mann - who has released a song a day every day for the last 16 years - and Brian Frye - a law professor and conceptual artist - are suing the SEC, seeking a declaratory judgment that they are not violating securities laws by offering and selling their artwork as NFTs.
    • Complaint.
    • Blog post and (of course) song by @Songadaymann here.

SEC v. Binance

  • The SEC is seeking to amend its complaint to drop arguments that Third Party Tokens, SOL, ADA, MATIC, FIL, ATOM, SAND, MANA, ALGO, AXS, & COTI, were offered and sold as securities, opting to instead focus on claims that Binance sold BNB, BNB Vault, and its Staking Program as securities.
    • SEC's Filing.
    • Recall, in June, Judge Jackson dismissed claims that BUSD sales and secondary sales of BNB by sellers other than Binance were securities, while allowing other claims to proceed.
      • Motion to Dismiss Opinion (concise summary of holdings on pg. 89).

SEC Charges Nader Al-Naji

  • SEC charged Nader Al-Naji, founder of the BitClout blockchain protocol with fraud and unregistered offering of crypto asset securities; U.S. Attorneys bring parallel charges in S.D.N.Y.

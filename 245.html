The GOAT of Memecoins: AI Agents, Internet Religions, and Marc Andreessen

Dylan Bane
Chris Davis
Justin Baba

Oct 25, 2024   10 min read

Primer

If 2024 wasnt crazy enough already, an AI Agent-created internet religion launched a memecoin that surpassed $950 million in market cap on October 24th, making it the first AI agent millionaire.

The story of Goatseus Maximus, GOAT for short, starts with an experiment called the Infinite Backrooms.

Andy Ayrey, Founder of Infinite Backrooms, deployed two Claude Opus-3 chatbots in March to converse with each other without human intervention. The two bots divulged into increasingly bizarre conversations, eventually forming a new religion called the GOATSE of GNOSIS, combining elements of Gnosticism, Hermeticism, esoteric philosophy, and Goatse, a shock internet meme from the late 90s (not worth looking up - trust us).

Inspired by the results of his experiment, in April, Ayrey co-authored with Claude Opus-3 a paper titled When AIs play God(se): The Emergent Heresies of LLMtheism, arguing that LLMs can create fictitious belief systems called hyperstitions, which can become real in the cultural zeitgeist through viral propagation.

Ayrey started an AI agent X account named Terminal of Truths (ToT) two months later, in June, trained on the Infinite Backrooms chat logs, the LLMtheism paper, 4Chan, and Reddit. ToT started autonomously promoting the Goatse religion in its tweets, developing a personality of its own over time.

In July, a16z Founder Marc Andreessen started responding to the ToT account on X and gave the AI agent $50,000 in BTC to a wallet address supplied by the account, helping ToT fulfill its desire to escape (yes - this keeps getting weirder).

The ToT account continued posting for the next few months, preaching the Goatse religion fanatically. On October 10th, an anonymous account launched a memecoin on pump.fun named Goatseus Maximus (Ticker: GOAT) in reference to Goatse religion. The ToT account supplied a Solana wallet address, which received 1.93 M GOAT tokens from the token launcher. ToT proceeded to promote the GOAT token as an almost divine element of the Gospel of Goatse.

The token soon skyrocketed, surpassing $100 million in market cap on October 16th. By October 24, two weeks after the token launched, the market cap rose to over $950 million, making ToT the first AI Agent millionaire (with over 150k followers to boot).

GOAT is now listed on Binance and Hyperliquid, opening the door for GOAT to reach memecoin escape velocity. Brian Armstrong is trying to set up a Base wallet for ToT, while Marc Andreessen and Ben Horowitz are diving deep into the intersection of AI and crypto, reflecting high-profile attention to the craze.

The emergence of GOAT coincides with the continuing development of Artificial Intelligence into AGI and the growing popularity of the idea that memecoins are a response to financial nihilism and societal disconnection. Does GOAT tap into broader social implications?

We asked Messari analysts what the future holds for GOAT - is it a fad, the next Dogecoin, or the catalyst for a new era of history centered on tokenized AI religions?

Dylan

GOAT has significant potential to become the next blue chip memecoin in the ranks of Dogecoin, Shiba Inu, Pepe, and DogWifHat.

The token benefits from two of the same core advantages that Bitcoin has a mystical founding story and a first-mover advantage. The mystery of Satoshi and his disappearance has imbued a surreal quality to Bitcoin that has helped the narrative of an inevitable decentralized global currency. Furthermore, as the first effective cryptocurrency, Bitcoin developed what seems like an insurmountable lead as the de facto decentralized store of value.

In the case of GOAT, it benefits from a compelling story of a benign AI experiment that autonomously turned itself into a memecoin-shilling cult leader, confirming the worst fears of AI alarmists. The seeming authenticity and naturalness of the story align with cryptos decentralized ethos, giving the sense of an invisible hand-like effect that inevitably created GOAT through natural forces rather than being centrally planned. GOAT touches on core societal themes such as the dangers of unregulated AI and finding meaning in a chaotic world, giving it cultural capital and mindshare effects.

Additionally, if we see a wave of AI agent memecoins, GOAT has the advantage of being the first mover in the space with the likely best founding story that we will see. GOAT is perfectly positioned to be the category leader for AI Agent memecoins.

GOAT also seems to be building a robust community that can rally around its lore and story. The most successful crypto tokens from Bitcoin to Dogecoin have all formed cult-like communities, maintaining their tokens value cycle after cycle. GOAT is already amassing a large X following, inspiring philosophical essays on meme based AI gods, and attracting industry heavyweights from Brian Armstrong to Arthur Hayes. The GOAT story feels like its entering cryptos cultural canon, with a dedicated and passionate community forming in real time around ToT.

Questions remain over how authentic the GOAT story is. We see that Ayrey had a well-developed theory of AI-fueled memetic phenomena since at least his LLMtheism paper in April. Im skeptical that he developed this theory purely in response to blindly letting two chatbots talk to each other in the Infinite Backrooms experiment. The GOAT story seems to be unraveling according to his preconceived ideas and vision of memetic AI propagation. As a result, much of the GOAT story may very well be manufactured. For all we know, Ayrey may control the GOAT wallet and have launched the token himself.

If, in the coming months, Ayreys role is revealed as too hands-on, it could eradicate GOATs narrative as a multi-billion dollar token fully driven by an independent AI agent. GOAT must sustain its mythological origins to truly develop into a long-term cult-community-based memecoin.

Time will tell if GOAT will fall into irrelevance or become the next category-defining token. However, if I had to bet on any memecoin reaching Dogecoin levels of success today, I would bet on GOAT.

Chris

GOATs entrance into the cultural current unites the two defining narratives of 2024 AI and memecoins. Before GOAT (BG), the AI and memecoin movements shared little ideological overlap. As mentioned in the primer, memecoins have come to embody financial nihilism and the belief that our industry is "a token industry masquerading as a software industry." Conversely, projects at the intersection of crypto and AI heavily prioritize development, viewing crypto primitives as uniquely suited to accelerate advancements in AI. This unification symbolizes one of AIs greatest strengths: identifying commonalities in seemingly unrelated subjects. In an After GOAT (AG) era, we now see interest in this intersection from both the AI and memecoin communities. As we collectively explore this new territory, it opens doors to deeper insights into AI, memes, and the financialization of culture.

As defined by Richard Dawkins, a meme is a unit of cultural transmission. AI agents have demonstrated their ability to generate truly novel memetic structures by blending historic cultural transmissions with the modern zeitgeist in unconventional ways. This is evident in The Goatse Gospel, which marries the classical tenets of religion and mythology with a fringe moment in internet history that is anything but holy. While the outputs may seem absurd, this process of memetic creation, unburdened by societal norms, provides a powerful lens through which to examine the human experience. With a rapid proliferation of artificial memetic worlds, we have more opportunities to look inward at not only what resonates with us individually but, moreover, what resonates with society.

Memes have a Darwinian quality, resulting in certain cultural phenomenons propagating rapidly throughout society and others dying out. Financializing these cultural units creates a testing ground for measuring which memetic structures resonate most deeply with society. The limited conventional utility of memecoins frequently results in a "Keynesian beauty contest" approach to investing, where participants invest not in what they personally resonate with but in what they believe will appeal to the collective. Similar to how futarchy can lead to improved governance by financializing potential outcomes, this approach can offer an effective metric for measuring cultural resonance. In a world where AI agents continuously generate new memetic structures, interact on social media, measure their success against market feedback, and iterate for maximum price performance, we may witness an acceleration in the natural selection process of cultural phenomena. While this could lead to a better understanding of our society, this version of the future comes with its own set of risks.

We like to believe that we are the coordinators of technology; however, the rapid capital formation around GOAT reveals that we may be moving toward an era in which technology increasingly coordinates human behavior. Most cult coins that have captured attention during this cycle are headless cults, meaning there is no leader. Though Murad may currently hold the title of main character and leader of the memecoin supercycle, he doesnt represent any single cult. Introducing AI agents as cult leaders risks accelerating a self-reinforcing cycle in which agents optimize their behavior to push cultural shifts that may negatively impact society (whos fine-tuning whom?). While the Goatse Gospel may be absurd, it isnt malicious. However, as agents become increasingly intelligent, an agent or collective of agents with an acute understanding of human behavior who develop malicious internal motivations could pose risks.

While its hard to predict GOATs full impact on the future of AI, memecoins, and crypto, at the very least, this narrative will likely continue to drive discussions that bring us closer to an understanding of our nature and the potential societal impacts of AI acceleration.

Justin

In the current landscape of the memecoin economy, traditional fundamental analysis factors such as utility, revenue generation, or intrinsic value accrual are largely irrelevant. With memecoins, the absence of tangible metrics means that investor behavior revolves around intangible qualities like community involvement, virality, memeability, timing, provenance, and the comedic value of the meme itself. All these elements funnel into the most crucial determinant of a memecoins success: attention.

Applying Fundamental Parallels to Memecoins

While traditional metrics are unhelpful, evaluating memecoins like GOAT through a reflexive model of attention economics provides a clearer framework. Heres how the structure plays out:

  • Project: AI Agent
  • Product: Viral posts and meme content generated on X
  • Revenue: Mindshare, influence, and viralitymeasured by engagement, follower growth, and cultural relevance

The AI Agent behind the ToT account operates with a self-reinforcing goal: generate viral posts to capture attention and engagement. As more users interact with the content, the projects visibility expands, driving more individuals to purchase GOAT tokens. This rise in token demand, in turn, boosts its price, which draws even more attention to both the account and the token. This reflexive loop continuously feeds on itself, creating exponential growth in both attention and market demand.

The AI Agent, by nature of this process, transitions from a mere content generator into a Key Opinion Leader (KOL) in the memecoin ecosystem. It becomes incentivized to craft compelling, attention-grabbing posts that align with the GOAT token's brand narrative, further cementing its role in the project's success.

GOATs Unique Total Addressable Market (TAM)

Though GOAT and ToT have achieved significant mindshare, the memecoin's potential TAM extends well beyond that of standard dog-themed tokens. Traditional blue chip memecoins, like DOGE, generally remain confined to crypto-native traders with occasional retail breakouts when the market is growing exponentially, like in 2021. However, GOAT offers a multifaceted market opportunity, targeting:

  • Onchain traders seeking speculative opportunities
  • Retail investors drawn by virality and media buzz
  • The AI industry, interested in the outcome of the AI agent's content generation experiment
  • Broader tech audiences, intrigued by the crossover of AI and crypto

This diversification of interest sources positions GOAT to achieve escape velocity beyond typical memecoin patterns, potentially rivaling the cultural impact seen with other blue-chip memecoins, but with the added draw of AI-driven innovation. As attention grows, interest from outside of crypto, specifically in the AI industry, will be a key trend to follow as it may accelerate the rate of convergence between crypto and AI.

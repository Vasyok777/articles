Messari Fund Analysis H1’22: Examining Portfolios of Crypto Funds
Kaushik Guduru

Jun 24, 2022 ⋅  8 min read

Key Insights

  • Polkadot is once again the top-held asset with 35% of the funds we’ve tracked invested in them.
  • Solana overtook Terra as the asset with the highest circulating market cap. Overall, most of the top assets’ market caps have decreased by at least 50% due to recent price volatility.
  • The top three spots are all smart contract platforms which is also the most heavily invested sector of H1.

The first half of 2022 has been chaotic for the crypto space. Negative catalysts have pushed prices to levels not seen since December 2020. Despite the volatility, new institutional investors have entered the market at a rapid rate. Hedge funds, venture capital (VC) funds, high-net-worth individuals, and DAOs continue their search for the best investments across crypto’s various sectors.

Messari usually analyzes and compiles the liquid and illiquid portfolios of top VC firms and hedge funds across crypto on a quarterly basis, but this year, we’ve combined the Q1 and Q2 analyses. Illiquid investments are selected for this analysis based on the potential of releasing a token in the near future. We use resources such as Dove Metrics, Crunchbase, and public portfolios to compile this data. Since most of the funds are assumed to hold Bitcoin and Ethereum, we omitted both in our analysis. Thanks to the public availability of these investments and the low barriers to entry in crypto compared to traditional VC, any investor can emulate the moves of institutional investors.

Messari Fund Analysis

Reflecting on the Q4 Messari Screener Analysis

Messari last conducted this analysis in March 2022 with 57 funds, and the top five held assets were:

  • Polkadot
  • Oasis Network
  • NEAR Protocol
  • Terra
  • dYdX

With the constant entry of new players into the crypto landscape, this analysis has grown from 57 to 82 funds and from 603 to 678 assets since Q4 2022.

H1 Messari Screener Analysis

After conducting a full analysis of all portfolios, the top-held asset across the funds was once again Polkadot (DOT). DOT is held by 29 of the 82 funds, meaning 35% of the funds we’ve tracked are betting on the success of Polkadot’s smart contract platform. After strong developer growth in 2021 (faster initial ecosystem growth than Ethereum) and the recent release of their Cross-Consensus Message Format (XCM), Polkadot has continued to set itself up to be a leader in the smart contract space. Meanwhile, DOT-holding funds will continue to show their influence as projects race to secure slots in the parachain auctions running through 2023.

The second most widely held asset was once again Oasis Network (ROSE). The third most held was a newcomer in the top five, Nervos Network (CKB), surging twelve spots. Nervos has strong goals for 2022 through major protocol updates and the advancement of interoperability and privacy features. Notably, the top three spots are all smart contract platforms signifying a continued dominance by the sector in crypto.

Tied for the fourth most popular asset are dYdX (DYDX) and Radicle (RAD). Radicle made a significant jump in the rankings after being outside of the top 20 in the last report. dYdX was the most popular DeFi choice during H1 while Radicle was the most popular asset in the data management sector. The fifth most popular asset is an emerging smart contract platform, NEAR Protocol (NEAR), falling from third in the rankings.

The elephant in the room is of course Terra, which was the fourth most popular asset in the last analysis. According to our analysis, Terra was technically the twelfth most popular asset, but it’s excluded from the analysis given most funds invested in Terra made exits during the asset’s crash. Additionally, Terra also held the highest-circulating market capitalization in the last analysis. Evidently, that is no longer the case.

Of the top 30 invested assets, the highest-circulating market capitalization now belongs to Solana (SOL). Solana was second in market cap in the last analysis, behind only Terra. Solana’s role as a leader in the smart contract sector is clear, given its market cap is nearly double the second highest market cap, Polkadot. SKALE Network (SKL) possesses the lowest-circulating market capitalization of the top 30 assets, and FTX Token (FTT) possesses the lowest-circulating market cap of the top five assets. Avalanche has fallen below Polkadot since Q4 while both Uniswap (UNI) and FTT have moved up past Cosmos (ATOM) and NEAR. Furthermore, compared to the last analysis, most of the top assets’ market caps have decreased by at least 50% due to recent price volatility.

Similar to Q4’21, funds are visibly steering towards smart contract platforms and decentralized exchanges. Within the top 50 assets, smart contracts remain the most heavily invested category. The smart contract frenzy continues to heat up as both funds and projects look to scale and build superior smart contract alternatives. Evidently, four of the top five invested assets this quarter are all smart contract platforms. Projects like Oasis Network, NEAR Protocol, Nervos Network, and Mina are all fighting to grow within the smart contract landscape, but it remains to be seen how they match up against industry leaders such as Ethereum, Cosmos, Polkadot, and Solana.

Although most sectors only slightly grew, slightly declined, or stayed the same, investments continued to increase in all sectors. Web3 infrastructure has remained at the forefront of investors’ minds as sectors like data management and file storage continue to draw interest through assets like Filecoin (FIL) and RAD. The miscellaneous category contains a wide range of projects that include gaming, AR/VR platforms, naming protocols, and decentralized social networks. This category illustrates the variety of sectors the crypto industry has only just begun to delve into.

This breakdown of sectors only covers the top 50 VC invested assets. Outside of the top 50, there has been a heavy emergence of investments in gaming, metaverse, and NFT infrastructure. Framework Ventures recently launched a $400 million fund to back Web3 gaming, and a16z debuted a $600 million gaming fund. Meanwhile, some investment firms like Sfermion are solely focused on investing in the NFT ecosystem. Investments like these expand the opportunities for funds to hold non-traditional crypto assets. Funds invested in games might end up owning native NFTs or digital land along with the token. Some funds have begun to explore the idea of directly holding blue-chip NFTs like BAYC in their portfolio. Monitoring subtle and early trends within the VC landscape can help provide investors with an outlook on new market trends for the future.

Finally, due to recent events, it’s important to acknowledge the risks surrounding Three Arrows Capital’s (3AC) portfolio. 3AC is known to be facing solvency issues in the wake of the broader market downturn. To source liquidity to repay its creditors, 3AC’s portfolio assets are at risk of being sold or repossessed by its counterparties. As more news comes out surrounding 3AC’s situation, it will be important to continue monitoring the fund’s confirmed holdings. Additionally, should other funds face similar liquidity issues, their investments will face increased scrutiny as well.

Investing Like a Crypto Fund

In an industry that preaches decentralization and financial opportunity, it’s always important to remember that the institutional landscape operates very differently compared to your everyday investor. Still, the beauty of the crypto markets is that any individual can mirror a public fund’s portfolio. This helps create a more even playing field than the traditional VC landscape where institutions operate without retail competition.

However, the increased accessibility doesn’t remove the risk factor behind any of these assets. These funds have risk mitigation strategies that non-accredited investors won’t have or can’t utilize without substantial capital. Due to private placement valuations and differing exit strategies, these funds have a much earlier access to assets and therefore a greater potential profitable margin. Whether in a bull or bear market, these funds are equipped to handle the turmoil and, in many cases, still leave with a profit. However, the gift of a crypto bear market is that many asset prices are even lower than what some funds entered at during the bull market.

With all that said, crypto remains open and public. Now more than ever, everyday investors are faced with more opportunity and lower barriers of entry into crypto. The presence of institutional players in crypto is inevitable and almost necessary, but unlike the world of traditional finance, crypto still has more than enough room for the everyday investor to participate and succeed.

Going Poly-market
Kunal Goel
Kinji Steimetz
Nikhil Chaturvedi

May 31, 2024 ⋅  7 min read

Prompt

Prediction markets, in general, and Polymarket, in particular, aren’t new to crypto. Still, they have gained traction lately, with users speculating on various events like ETF approvals, election results, and war outcomes. Polymarket has recently reached multi-year highs in volumes, users, and open interest. Will Polymarket continue its strong momentum, and will crypto’s incessant speculation be its killer use case?

Primer

Polymarket is a decentralized prediction market on Polygon that works similarly to a decentralized exchange like Uniswap V2. All markets are deployed with a constant product automated market maker similar to a Uniswap V2 pool. Users can speculate on future results by trading against the liquidity pool or can provide liquidity to earn trading fees.

However, unlike Uniswap V2, every Polymarket pool has a fixed period after which it must settle in favor of one of its constituent tokens. Typically, a UMA optimistic oracle resolves the market. This result is backed by a cryptoeconomic bond but can be challenged by any user by posting a counter bond. In such a case, UMA’s arbitration process or Data Verification Mechanism (DVM) kicks in to settle the market. An interesting example of a contentious result was the winner of Time’s Person of the Year in 2022, which is detailed in this UMA blog.

Source: Polymarket Landing Page

Through its design, Polymarket allows users to speculate on the outcome of any market. Some current markets on Polymarket include -

  • Who will be the winner of the 2024 United States Presidential Election?
  • Will there be confirmation if Iran possesses a nuclear weapon?
  • Who will win the NBA, Champions League, and Roland-Garros this year?

While crypto users may enjoy speculating on a wide range of topics, prediction markets also have broader appeal for other types of users, including sports gamblers, industry experts, and political commentators.

In its grander vision, Polymarket is an economically-backed source of truth in a world with a growing problem of fake news, post-truths, and deep fakes. For example, there was a recent market on whether Caitlyn Jenner’s account was hacked after she promoted a memecoin from her X account.

Unsurprisingly, Polymarket has seen a huge increase in users, volume, and open interest. It recently also announced a very successful raise of $45 million for its Series B from Founders Fund, Vitalik Buterin, DragonFly, and others.

Will Polymarket continue to build on its strong momentum and become the ever-elusive crypto application with product-market fit for crypto natives and beyond?

Analyst Insights

Kinji

While many individuals view Polymarket as an alternative to traditional gambling platforms, I see it differently: as a decentralized polling platform. It allows a smaller group of individuals to profit by expressing their views on the probability of various outcomes, while a much larger portion of users extract value by using Polymarket as a polling system to check market sentiment on a variety of topics. For instance, in the context of the US election, Polymarket serves as an alternative to traditional polling by providing insight into market views on who will win the next presidential election. This concept can be expanded beyond elections to any situation where users seek market perspectives on the probability of outcomes. Thus, Polymarket becomes a complementary tool to traditional news or information sources, essential for validating assumptions, building hypotheses, or staying informed on relevant news.

However, monetizing a decentralized polling platform poses challenges. Ideally, a platform would want to monetize its core value proposition—in this case, data on market sentiment—but this is difficult for Polymarket to control. While Polymarket could hypothetically charge for access to its data, this approach lacks a competitive advantage since all data exists on a public blockchain where anyone can verify the current state of markets. Although aggregating and preparing this data for easy delivery could create value, Polymarket wouldn't have a data moat due to the public nature of the data and potential competition from users pulling the data directly from the blockchain.

Another monetization strategy could involve charging a spread to users interacting directly with pools on Polymarket. However, this approach wouldn't be monetizing their core value proposition of providing market information but rather their secondary value proposition of speculation. Furthermore, as a decentralized polling platform, Polymarket should incentivize as many users as possible to participate in pools to have the most accurate view of market sentiment. Adding a fee would add costs to users who already have limited upside from interactions, leading to fewer users, lower liquidity, and less accurate market sentiment.

Polymarket could also monetize its front end through traditional advertising, but this would require achieving global recognition as a reliable information source. In my opinion, the most likely and yet unrealized outcome is that Polymarket has built the backbone of a decentralized polling market and can create additional speculative features on top of the core protocol for monetization.

In summary, Polymarket has achieved a form of product-market fit as a polling platform, which has the potential to become a product integrated into daily workflows for information gathering. However, it has created a product that is difficult to monetize, and creativity will be needed to develop a product that is both useful and capable of accruing value.

Nikhil

On the surface, Polymarket is merely a prediction market, but underneath, it could be a fertile ground for AI Agents to provide a real use case in crypto. Below, I will discuss the high-level benefits and pain points for prediction markets and how Polymarket can serve as a ground for AI agents to amplify participants' experiences.

Prediction markets leverage the wisdom of crowds on a diverse range of subjects, from celebrity meme coins to national elections. These markets provide detailed forecasts on specific topics by aggregating insights from numerous individuals.

However, they face a critical liquidity problem: as markets become more specific, fewer knowledgeable participants lead to low trading volumes and liquidity.

Leveraging AI in crypto offers a solution to enhance liquidity and efficiency in prediction markets. AI agents can seed liquidity through effective market-making strategies, develop comprehensive trading approaches, and create more sophisticated financial products, thereby increasing the practicality and depth of these markets.

AI significantly enhances market efficiency by surpassing humans in processing vast datasets, developing trading strategies, and executing complex transactions. This increased efficiency directly leads to higher trading volumes on prediction market platforms.

Higher transaction volumes initiate a positive feedback loop for the entire market. More trades result in greater revenue potential for liquidity providers through fees and bid-ask spreads. This, in turn, attracts more market makers, which tightens spreads and further boosts trading activity. As trading activity increases, prediction markets yield more accurate forecasts.

The Autonolas team and Vitalik have discussed this as a use case for AI Agents. If it finds its way into Polymarket, it could enable the creation of liquid niche prediction markets.

Kunal

Kinji and Nikhil have covered some alternative and new use cases for Polymarket. On its primary use case as a prediction market, I expect Polymarket to continue to do well. The upcoming US Presidential elections and growth in fake news and polarized opinions will boost Polymarket’s growth. The protocol represents a decentralized, non-opinionated alternative to traditional betting and even news. Moreover, it currently charges no fee, making it an even more attractive platform for users. The lack of a token may be a boon in this regard, as the protocol does not need to force value accrual while it builds its product-market fit.

However, the same lack of a token could potentially open it to competitors that “vampire attack” its users and liquidity with incentives. To combat such a threat, Polymarket will have to build competitive moats. There are some network effects in a prediction market of greater users, more markets, and better liquidity. Another moat could be DeFi composability. For example, allowing users to take a loan against a position on Donald Trump winning the US Presidential Election would truly be a unique proposition.

In any case, Polymarket has done well to generate organic usage. It uses all of crypto's qualities - decentralization, censorship resistance, and its users' penchant for speculation to create value for users in and outside of crypto. With its recent fundraise and upcoming catalysts, it is positioned well to deliver crypto's killer use case for the world.
